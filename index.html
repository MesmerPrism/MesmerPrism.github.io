Mesmer Prism

Mesmer Prism is the public home for work that moves between altered perception, weakly bounded bodies, and the tools needed to study them. The live systems are Quest and XR projects: Viscereality, Rusty XR, companion tooling, and reusable Quest documentation boundaries. The public web and citizen-science side now includes the Altered Xperience Project with CIRCE. The writing and research side follows imagery variation, prophantasia, scientific surrealism, patterned light, and plasmatic embodiment. The common rule is simple: strange experience is taken seriously, but claims stay tied to mechanisms, sources, and buildable tests.

Many entries are active research maps rather than finished literature reviews. They clarify sources, terminology, and claim boundaries in fragmented areas. Stronger claims should be read as provisional unless a project is explicitly marked as finished and reviewed.

Background

My background moves between cognitive science, HCI, psychophysics, media theory, and research writing. The writing side names concepts, lineages, and claim boundaries before carrying those questions into XR systems, browser simulations, physiological sensing, and embodied prototypes.

Across Plasmatic Multitudes, SANE, Tulpas, Scientific Surrealism, Rusty XR, Quest Companion Tools, Hollow Face Lab, Altered Xperience Project, Brain Candy, Strobotorch, Bressloff V1 Form Constants, Deep Dream, Optical Movement Illusions, Phenomenological Control, Prophantasia, and Fractal Optics, the recurring terrain is altered perception, weakly bounded bodies, phenomenological variation, and the practical conditions that make strange experience legible without flattening it.
