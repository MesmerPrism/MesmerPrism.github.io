Immersive systems, research prototypes, and interactive design.

I build Unity/C# applications and interdisciplinary prototypes across virtual reality, biofeedback, HCI, perception, and embodiment.

My current work focuses on Quest-based XR systems, breath and biofeedback interaction, experimental interfaces, and research tooling that sits between engineering, design, and empirical investigation. I have an M.Sc. in Cognitive Science and I work best on projects that need both technical implementation and conceptual clarity.

Background

My background combines cognitive science, HCI, psychophysics, embodied interaction, and applied XR development. I do not treat writing and engineering as separate lanes. In practice, I move between system building, design framing, prototype evaluation, and public-facing explanation.

I am usually most useful when a project sits between disciplines and still needs to become concrete: a new XR interaction, a biofeedback pipeline, an experimental interface, a technical narrative that needs to become buildable, or a prototype that needs to become legible to both collaborators and users.

Core strengths

  • Unity and C# for XR and interactive systems
  • Standalone Quest deployment and performance tuning
  • Biofeedback, breath interaction, and interoception-oriented design
  • Rapid prototyping for unusual interfaces and embodied interaction
  • HCI, psychophysics, and research-oriented evaluation
