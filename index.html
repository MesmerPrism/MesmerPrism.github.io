Mesmer Prism

Mesmer Prism is my public map for work that moves between altered perception, weakly bounded bodies, and the tools needed to study them. The live systems are Quest and XR projects: Viscereality, Rusty XR, companion tooling, and the Meta passthrough source map. The public web and citizen-science side now includes the Altered Xperience Project with CIRCE. The writing and research side follows imagery variation, prophantasia, scientific surrealism, patterned light, and plasmatic embodiment. The common rule is simple: strange experience is taken seriously, but claims stay tied to mechanisms, sources, and buildable tests.

Important caveat: my writing projects are primarily maintained with AI assistance. Their default purpose is to be a public window into investigations in progress and a comprehensive source and terminology map for areas that are often fragmented. Unless a page is explicitly marked as finished and reviewed, do not read it as a final literature review, a claim that every claim or wording has been manually reviewed, or an attempt to persuade you of every opinion expressed there.

Background

My background moves between cognitive science, HCI, psychophysics, media theory, and research writing. I use writing to name concepts, lineages, and claim boundaries before carrying those questions into XR systems, browser simulations, physiological sensing, and embodied prototypes.

Across Plasmatic Multitudes, SANE, Scientific Surrealism, Rusty XR, Quest Companion Tools, Altered Xperience Project, Meta Passthrough Stack, Brain Candy, Strobotorch, Deep Dream, Optical Movement Illusions, Phenomenological Control, Prophantasia, and Fractal Optics, I keep returning to altered perception, weakly bounded bodies, phenomenological variation, and the practical conditions that make strange experience legible without flattening it.

Core strengths

  • Unity and C# for XR and interactive systems
  • Biofeedback, breath interaction, and interoception-oriented design
  • Perception research, psychophysics, and embodied HCI
  • Citizen-science web infrastructure, survey tooling, and public research guides

  • Weakly bounded embodiment, avatar design, and plasmatic interaction
