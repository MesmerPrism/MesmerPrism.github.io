Writing and conceptual synthesis

SANE

SANE is the working framework I use to coordinate four dimensions that otherwise tend to drift apart: the somaesthetic, the anthropotechnical, the noetic, and the emergent. It is the project where inner ecology, technics, mediation, pedagogy, and morphogenetic thinking are being pulled into one practical vocabulary for coupling practice, tools, narrative, and collective patterning.

Framework

What the acronym is doing

The somaesthetic dimension keeps the framework grounded in lived bodily practice, cultivation, and attention. The anthropotechnical dimension keeps technics, tools, interfaces, and educational or pharmacological supports inside the model rather than treating them as neutral containers. The noetic dimension tracks symbolic, social, narrative, and inner-dialogic processes. The emergent dimension keeps the system open to multi-scale dynamics, morphogenesis, and the way patterns can become real through repetition, coupling, and coordination.

Publicly, the work is still early. I do not want to publish it yet as a finalized doctrine. This page frames it as a structured attempt to build a critical and practice-facing language for inner ecology, technical mediation, and collective sense-making.

Guardrails

Boundaries

SANE keeps distance from cosmic explanation and mood-board synthesis. It makes body practice, AI, ritual, pedagogy, and systems theory comparable without pretending they are identical, and it keeps relation, imagination, and technical mediation inside a disciplined structure.

Source control matters here because the framework currently rests on a mix of stabilized anchors and still-partial humanities sources. The public page should therefore read as a map of active work, not as a final manifesto.

Public focus

  • Framework clarity over maximal ideological scope
  • Lineage and vocabulary control over premature proclamation
  • Practice, mediation, and emergence as interacting dimensions rather than isolated themes
