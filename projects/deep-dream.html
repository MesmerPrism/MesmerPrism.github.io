Simulated psychedelic vision

Deep Dream

Deep Dream is the project where I am trying to say, as precisely as possible, what hallucination-machine systems do and do not simulate. The work combines machine vision, VR, cyberdelic design, community replication cultures, and a recurring problem in altered-perception research: how to model unusual phenomenology without flattening it into hype.

Direction

What the project clarifies

DeepDream can support several different similarity claims: phenomenological resemblance, questionnaire overlap, partial neural convergence, controllable stimulus classes for experiments, and cyberdelic design history. Those claims need separate names because the field often slides between them too quickly.

This project therefore treats simulation as graded. It tracks the lineage from Inceptionism and community video-stabilization hacks into the Hallucination Machine literature, then asks where the evidence is strongest, where stimulus provenance or rights issues matter, and which mismatch cases remain unresolved.

Current work

  • Historical lineage from Google DeepDream to VR hallucination platforms
  • Phenomenological fidelity and questionnaire-matching claims
  • EEG, entropy, and cognitive-flexibility results under DeepDream exposure
  • Media provenance, replication culture, and stimulus-pipeline transparency

Connected projects

  • Brain Candy for broader non-pharmacological state-shift design
  • Phenomenological Control for imagery, expectancy, and visionary cognition
  • Optical Movement Illusions for induced-vision mechanism questions outside machine vision

Boundary

Useful limits

Deep Dream is strongest when it refuses the all-or-nothing trap. It is already a valuable model of altered visual phenomenology, a concrete cyberdelic design history, and a test case for how machine priors get projected back onto sensory input. Those uses matter without an equivalence claim to pharmacological psychedelics.

The project is also a reminder that stimulus engineering is part of the method. Parameter choices, stabilization pipelines, optical-flow inheritance, and rights provenance all shape what kind of hallucination object is actually being studied.

Public focus

  • Graded simulation claims instead of totalizing rhetoric
  • Explicit separation of fidelity, outcome, and provenance questions
  • Cyberdelics as a historical and technical design line
