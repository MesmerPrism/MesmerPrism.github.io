Altered perception and guided state shifts

Brain Candy

Brain Candy is best understood as a research-informed non-pharmacological platform for guided state shifts. The project sits where rhythmic visual stimulation, fractal fluency, expectancy-sensitive framing, immersive design, and interactive regulation start to overlap in a way that can be built and tested. Fractal fluency means the relative ease with which visual systems process fractal structure.

Direction

What the project tests

Brain Candy treats altered perception as a design problem with specific variables: controllable visual rhythm, abstract multiscale structure, bioresponsive pacing, and context design. The project stays useful when those variables are tested directly, without importing claims the evidence cannot support.

The current archive gives the project a relatively disciplined shape. Rhythmic visual stimulation and flicker-hallucination research provide one mechanistic anchor. Fractal fluency and restorative abstraction provide another. The cyberdelic and phenomenological-control lines explain why framing, suggestion, and onboarding are part of the mechanism rather than superficial packaging.

Current work

  • Rhythmic stimulation and controlled visual perturbation
  • Fractal fluency, symmetry, and restorative abstraction
  • Expectation, liminal framing, and cyberdelic context design
  • Interactive regulation through breathing, music, and contingent feedback

Connected projects

  • Deep Dream for hallucination simulation and cyberdelic comparison
  • Fractal Optics for patterned light, fractal stimulation, and comparator evidence
  • Viscereality for bioresponsive VR and closed-loop immersive interaction

Boundary

Evidence boundary

Brain Candy becomes more useful when the claims stay narrow. Its strongest public rationale is a platform for designing and measuring state shifts around rhythm, geometry, attention, valence, and user-guided regulation.

The restorative and fractal-visual lines are treated as early design hypotheses. The page uses them to justify careful comparison conditions, not therapeutic promises.

The current public framing therefore emphasizes comparison conditions, parameter logging, and structured outcomes. The design question asks which variables changed, what they are meant to do, and what counts as restoration, regulation, or altered visual phenomenology in a measurable way.

Public focus

  • Guided non-pharmacological state shifts
  • Design variables that can be tuned and tested
  • Context engineering rather than hype-heavy consciousness language
