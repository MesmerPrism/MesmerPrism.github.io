Altered perception and guided state shifts

Brain Candy

Brain Candy is a research-informed non-pharmacological platform for guided state shifts and psychedelic-like visual phenomenology. The current archive frames it as a state-input-participant problem: a structured visual and auditory input meets a participant's current state, expectations, attention, and tolerance. The project sits where rhythmic visual stimulation, fractal fluency, recursive visual structure, immersive framing, music, breath, and interactive regulation overlap in a way that can be built and tested. Fractal fluency means the relative ease with which visual systems process fractal structure.

Direction

What the project tests

Brain Candy treats altered perception as a design problem with specific variables: controllable visual rhythm, abstract multiscale structure, bioresponsive pacing, and context design. The current research map makes that more explicit by separating input properties from state reports and participant differences. The project stays useful when those variables are tested directly, without importing claims the evidence cannot support.

The working archive gives the project a relatively disciplined shape. Rhythmic visual stimulation and flicker-hallucination research provide one mechanistic anchor, now with measurement work around stroboscopic phenomenology, 6D-VHQ, MOSAIC topic modelling, and ASC questionnaire infrastructure. Fractal fluency and restorative abstraction provide another. Psychedelic visual-science work, including visual contextual computation and connectome-harmonic comparisons, helps place recursive, multiscale, and symmetry-rich imagery against known visual-system levels without treating the comparison as settled validation. The cyberdelic, music, dream-engineering, and phenomenological-control lines explain why framing, suggestion, onboarding, audio, and state context are part of the mechanism rather than superficial packaging.

Boundary

Evidence boundary

Brain Candy is clearest when the claims stay narrow. Its strongest public rationale is a platform for designing and measuring state shifts around rhythm, geometry, attention, valence, and user-guided regulation.

The restorative, fractal-visual, timed-light, and whole-brain-dynamics lines are treated as early design hypotheses or comparator literatures. The page uses them to justify careful comparison conditions, not therapeutic promises.

The current public framing therefore emphasizes comparison conditions, parameter logging, and structured outcomes. Flicker, fractal exposure, attention restoration, and rhythmic regulation are treated as separable design families. The design question asks which variables changed, what they are meant to do, and what counts as restoration, regulation, or altered visual phenomenology in a measurable way. Brain Candy does not claim to treat ADHD, depression, anxiety, trauma, or dementia, and 40/60 Hz comparator work is not presented as proof of product effects.

Public focus

  • Guided non-pharmacological state shifts
  • Design variables that can be tuned and tested
  • Context engineering rather than hype-heavy consciousness language
  • No clinical efficacy claims without dedicated trials
